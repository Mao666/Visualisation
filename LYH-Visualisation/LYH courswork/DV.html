Most non-EU graduates go home after their studies, but some graduates might consider the possibility of working in UK after their studies in the UK and then possibly settlement in the UK. How many non-EU students could truly settle in after they graduate from UK?


Follow the law the UK, people could settle in the UK after five years of continuous lawful residence. Therefore, let us see how many Non-EU student could settle in the UK 5 year later by using study Visa firstly.
Over two-thirds (68%) of migrants who were issued a Study visa in 2004 no longer had valid leave to remain in the UK 5 years later. This proportion has increased in each subsequent cohort to 86% in 2011. For people issued a Study visa in 2004, 3.1% had gained settlement 5 years later and a further 29% still had valid leave to stay in the UK, either as students or in another route. For those arriving in 2011, only 0.3% had gained settlement 5 years later and a further 14% still had valid leave to stay in the UK. It is clearly see the rate of settlement has declined steadily. This change are consistent with the tightening of the Immigration Rules for students since September 2007.
